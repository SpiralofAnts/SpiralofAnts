So, the events I have in mind happen over a long span of time, but the story itself starts being told in roughly the early 2010s. Although, technically the story is only being told two to three loops in- but I'm getting ahead of myself.

Okay, so- by the way, if this wasn't obvious already, this is a "furry" story (I'm writing it no differently than I would if they were all humans, it makes no difference to me, the almighty author, but I feel like its important to note because some people have bad taste, but anyways, imagine a mouse boy, age 21, Alucard, and all his young adult friends:

A lion, age 21, named Chris who stands double Al's height (Al is ever so slightly shorter than 3 feet tall, Chris is half an inch below 6 feet tall) and is an absolute sweetheart; a purple cat named Oliver, only 19, but very much a druggy; Allegra, a goth doe who looks to be about 19; Crush, an orange vampire squid, age 23, also happens to be her boyfriend; and Ares, a rather timid sea otter who mixes all of their audio.

Oh, did I not mention?? By the way, they're all in a band together. Al's on keys and digital instruments, Chris and Ollie are both drummers, though Oliver is really just Chris's sub for when he can't make it, Allegra is their bassist, Crush is their lead guitarist and lead vocalist, and, as previously mentioned, Ares works the technical side of things, kinda acts as a roadie for them as he sets up all their equipment.

The timeline starts with these six people, then one day a weird kid starts showing up to their band practices and just sorta watches. Doesn't seem to super love the music, and even gets physically uncomfortable hearing Crush's guitar make high-pitched whiney tones, but he sticks around. Long story short, this kid is actually an alien sent to observe the six, but specifically Alucard, but won't really elaborate as to why, other than that his boss, SMITE (super-massive integrated thought entity), is made up of all thoughts, past, present, and future, and Toya was sent there for a very good reason.

Turns out, the reason he was sent there is because one of Al's "friends" (he's friendly with Alucard but secretly doesn't really care for him) manages to get a complete sample of his DNA. This friend (named Pavlov btw) is also working for a shadow-y, pseudo-government agency known only as "The Committee". The reason he gets Al's DNA is kinda random, it's just because he's a scientist working for The Committee and he happens to know Al and think he'd make a great test subject. So, armed with his DNA, The Committee engineers a clone of him and puts Pavlov in charge of taking care of him. A home environemnt is recreated and emulated in the lab, but without any other actual kids around, the clone (dubbed "Albert" but known internally as "MCP-A1") turns out like a politically extreme version of Alucard. Well, kinda sorta, I wanted to say "evil" but then I realized it's very hard for me to call someone inherently evil, I dunno.

Anyways, point is that Albert is successfully cloned, and grows up at the same rate, so by the time he's 21, the original Alucard would be 42. Over this very long 21 years, Pavlov gained the trust of not only his cloned surrogate son, but also of The Committee, and was promoted to department head roughly five years before Albert's 21st birthday. Albert, unknown to him, is groomed to be the sort of perfect cynical person to take head of The Committee, but it's not like the current head of committee would just step down or let his position be usurped. Although a wealthy CEO and usually surrounded by armed guards, not all events can be planned for. Pavlov's department put some funding aside for secret orders, secret orders made out to put a hit on the current head of committee. Mind you, any given member of The Committee only deals with those directly beneath them, so only a few people knew the identity of this person and what his assassination would mean to The Committee. Obviously suspicion was thrown around, but with no proof of anything, it was equally likely that all the department heads had something to do with his demise. As a precaution, they were all barred from ever becoming the head of committee themselves, but this is exactly where Albert comes into Pavlov's plan. He already has the poor kid wrapped around his finger, all he needs to do is present him like he's not just a company asset, but someone viable to run it, and he's essentially in charge through his manipulation of Albert!

So, spoiler warning, that does come to pass, and on Albert's 21st birthday, he inherents control of The Committee. By this point, it's at least 203X when Albert takes control, and using his wide shadow-y influence, he slowly turns all of the USA's citizens into effectively slaves through increased militarization and defunding of social programs, while encouraging businesses to maximize profits. Essentially, if you live in the USA at this time, you're stuck in the gears of capitalism with no choice but to keep grinding yourself to the bone. Great stuff, really!

You may be wondering "uh, what does that mean for the previous six characters you were talking about? Doesn't this seem kinda unrelated?" and yes! It may seem that way indeed, but these stories intersect again, I swear! The band never really takes off, so they're not really in the public eye when they all happen to be near the site of a meteorite crashing down into earth. Luckily for them, they're not hit by anything but the shockwave, but a few of 'em still end up injured as they ended up pinned under a car. Just Crush and Al, but still, it shook them enough to the point where Al sorta became a hermit and Crush became bitter over how he lost function of his right arm, couldn't play guitar anymore. They all go their seperate ways (for the most part), with Crush and Allegra engaged but not legally married (the Why will become obvious later), and nobody really keeping up with each other. Chris tries to keep in touch and sends everyone the occasional message on holidays or birthdays, but doesn't really talk to them outside of that- except for Al, who he sticks around and motivates into pursuing a passion of his: mad science!

Now, it should be noted that what Alucard and Chris get up to isn't really "science" per se but moreso happy accidents in an apartment they call a laboratory- at least for the first year or so. Then, Alucard got into computers and actually went to college to learn a thing or two! He didn't actually graduate, but hey! He stepped away with a far more advanced understanding of circuitry and components and could actually make electronics now!! This didn't actually mean much for the lab, except bigger costs with still 0 profits. Still, Chris and Al poured all the extra money from their dayjobs into doing stupid wacky creative shit with whatever was around, and eventually Al and his lab assistant got pretty good at whipping stuff up in surprisingly little time! Most of the things Al invents aren't really practically useful, but they're fun to mess around with. Really, he should've been selling his designs as toys, but even then, they'd be kinda dangerous toys for kids. In any case, they never really made a career out of their skills, just dicked around.

That is, until capitalism start caving in around them. By the time they noticed their free time dwindling to shreds and their relative bank accounts' worths shrinking rather than growing, it was illegal to leave the country. Though it was increasingly obvious they were being boiled alive like that poor frog in that experiment, they noticed and simply couldn't do anything! Well, until of course they stumbled onto time travel.

Complete dumb luck, by the way! They don't so much invent time travel as they discover an individual capable of it, sort of... Essentially, that accident that happened years ago where a meteorite crashed into the earth not far from where they were (hiking on a mountain, though they hadn't gone more than twenty feet from their car yet) introduced a new sickness to the earth. They all developed fevers, some worse than others, in the two weeks after the meteorite hit the mountain. They didn't really attribute it to the accident, just an unrelated cold that swept through the band at a similar time- Well, aside from Allegra. She never caught it, for some reason. After they got over their sicknesses though, they all developed strange abilities. Some noticed theirs immediately- Alucard and Ares notably, but both hid their powers in fear they'd be shunned. To be fair, all Alucard got was the ability to see red strings coming out of everybody's heads, and Ares got the exciting ability to... levitate things in his hands and spin them really, really fast. Technically, he could alter the density, angular momentum, and electric charge of any object he could fit in the palm of his hand, but he didn't see a lot of use in making handsaws only he could use, other than freaking people out. Hence, hiding his ability for decades. The other members, after eventually discovering their abilities, did very similar things and simply didn't talk about them to anybody- until Ares visited Al and Chris's lab out of nowhere one day!

Hearing about Ares's ability, Al and Chris immediately got the idea to try and time travel. See, while time travel was theoretically possible, it required really specific testing parameters that weren't really possible as far as they knew. But, Ares could theoretically generate a black hole in the palm of his hand. All he would need to do is make something so dense that it collapses in on itself! Ideally while it's spinning really fast, obviously, and with the ability to change the charge from positive, neutral, or negative freely, they found they could do some real testing!! Granted, nothing was really big enough to fit through the black hole aside from digital information, so they ended up confirming time travel worked and existed by sending themselves texts. Unfortunately, they found something that ended their testing pretty quickly. While time travel was possible, it also seemed to confirm the many worlds theory, as their timeline wouldn't actually change. Alucard sent himself winning lottery numbers and, despite his past self knowing he was working on time travel, his current self wasn't crazy rich. The conclussion they drew was that they could only really affect timelines that weren't their own. Neat! But kinda useless. They did however get time travel down to a science. It takes a hot second to get the black hole to the proper charge, but essentially, the more negative charge it has, the further into the past the message is sent. Theoretically, they could also send messages into the future by using positive charge, but they found that was easy enough already by setting alarms. Granted- sending a message to the future the short way round was less energy- not that they tested that ever. Simply theoretical.

After enough testing though, The Committee did notice, though only sometime after Albert had taken control. Within a month, their lab had been bugged and Ares's ability discovered. Ares was assassinated long after that, poisoned at a restaurant. A text meant to deter Al and Chris was sent their way, but only served to motivate them to start working in absolute secret. Late at night, they'd show up to where they used to practice. Thankfully, the garage was still owned by the same people who never checked up on their properties. This unmonitored space allowed Alucard and Chris to work secretly on a rather grim project, though one that did end up working in their favor. Y'see, while it's been proven that things can come back to life if you simply have machinery act as their heart and lungs, it's rather frowned upon to try and bring people back to life after they die. This was not enough of a deterrent for Alucard, though Chris lost faith in the experimentation pretty quickly. They never actually got his brain working again, or any of his internal organs really, but they did manage to keep his shell around. They rather slowly replaced his bones with a thin metal skeleton, turning him into an extremely human-like robot. The one thing they kept aside from the skin was his eyes. Alucard was no biologist and had no actual intention of patching biological material into his robotic design, he simply thought it would look weird for his invention to use the skin of a man and have mechanical eyes. So, although they are cold, unfeeling, and unseeing, the robot he designed can blink and even turn the eyes. There are occular replacement parts buried in the eyes, it's just that they're being used for aesthetic purposes only. Despite being fully robotic- a walking, talking coffin, really - this robot was programmed to refer to himself as an android- a marriage between machine and biology. He was not. He was, however, impressive in that he had his own digital life.

And, for whatever odd reason, placing all his circuitry within Ares's corpse actually did appear to have an effect on him. He was not biological, not in the slightest, but somehow he managed to inheret Ares's ability- an ability granted to him by a physical illness. How odd. How impossible! And yet, it still happened. Seymour, as the robot would dub himself, had a differing ability however. Rather than being able to turn small objects into black holes, it was an internal mechanism of his that was wound into a black hole- and wasn't destroyed by the effect either! Actually, it had the effect of pulling the entire robot through spacetime, and seemingly safely too! While Seymour purports that he can transport other people through time with him if they were only holding hands, neither Al or Chris were brave enough to test this theory themselves.
